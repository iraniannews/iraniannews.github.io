Iran and Six World Powers Reach Historic Agreement on Nuclear Framework

April 24, 2026  •  By ISNN Staff Correspondent  •  Vienna

After weeks of intensive negotiations held behind closed doors in Vienna, diplomats representing Iran and the six major world powers announced Thursday a landmark preliminary accord that would see the Islamic Republic significantly curtail its uranium enrichment activities in exchange for a broad package of sanctions relief, opening what observers called a new chapter in Iran's relationship with the international community.

The agreement, reached in the early hours of the morning following an all-night session, was hailed by Iranian Foreign Minister Ali Bagheri as a victory for diplomacy and dialogue. "This accord demonstrates that through mutual respect and determined engagement, the most complex disputes can find resolution at the negotiating table," he told reporters outside the Palais Coburg hotel where the talks had been conducted.

Key Terms of the Framework

Under the terms of the preliminary deal, Iran has agreed to limit uranium enrichment to levels consistent with civilian energy production and to allow expanded inspections by international monitors. In return, the major powers pledged to lift a significant portion of the economic sanctions that have weighed on Iran's economy for more than a decade, with relief taking effect in phases as Iran fulfills its commitments.

Senior negotiators cautioned that the framework still requires translation into a full technical agreement, a process expected to take several additional weeks. Both sides expressed confidence that the hardest questions had now been resolved and that remaining details were workable.

"What we have achieved today is not merely a technical agreement — it is a demonstration that the world can choose dialogue over confrontation."

Reactions in Tehran were broadly positive, with state media reporting celebrations in several major cities. The government called an emergency session of parliament for Friday to brief lawmakers on the accord's provisions.

Regional Implications

Analysts expect the deal to have significant reverberations across the broader Middle East, potentially easing tensions in several regional flashpoints and opening the door to renewed economic ties between Iran and European trading partners who had largely withdrawn from the Iranian market following the re-imposition of sanctions several years ago.

Further details on the implementation timeline and the specific sanctions relief packages are expected to be released in an official joint statement later this week.
